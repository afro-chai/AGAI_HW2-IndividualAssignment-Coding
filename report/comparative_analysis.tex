\begin{abstract}
\noindent This work implements a small multi-agent \textbf{StockTrader} pipeline: two graded strategies---\textbf{News Sentiment Follower} and \textbf{Volatility Averse}---plus a third \textbf{Moral Trader} extension, orchestrated with Microsoft \textbf{AutoGen} and local \textbf{Ollama} inference. Each symbol is analyzed in parallel from the same market snapshot; an evaluator then summarizes agreement and tension across agents. The study uses a compact basket of five U.S.\ listings chosen for sector spread and defense--geopolitics exposure, including Africa--MENA energy and gold mining angles alongside defense and shipping names. A lightweight news sentiment feed supplies optional headlines; a separate historical exercise scores transparent stand-ins for the two graded philosophies against realized forward returns without replaying the LLMs on past dates. 
\section{Strategy Selection and Rationale}

\noindent The two graded strategies I implemented are \textbf{News Sentiment Follower} (Strategy~A) and \textbf{Volatility Averse} (Strategy~B). I paired them because they differ on \textbf{what counts as evidence}: Strategy~A privileges external narrative and sentiment when available, while Strategy~B treats realized turbulence, drawdown depth, and stability of the tape as first-class risk signals. I chose them because of the inherent applicability to the defense sector.

\textbf{Defense--geopolitics framing.} The default run uses a \textbf{small, diverse} basket with explicit \textbf{AFRICOM} hooks on \textbf{TTE} (Africa--MENA oil and LNG) and \textbf{GOLD} (Mali / DRC gold exposure narratives), plus U.S.\ defense software, a prime, and tanker beta.

\textbf{Extension.} \textbf{Moral Trader} adds a third parallel branch for future accountability, evaluated only after A and B finish for the same ticker in hopes of future final project incorporation.

\textbf{Expected disagreement (before running).} I expected the sharpest splits where \emph{(i)} headline sentiment is missing or stale but price paths are extreme, because News Sentiment must lean on sparse momentum proxies while Volatility Averse still reads realized turbulence and drawdowns; \emph{(ii)} momentum looks strong yet short-horizon volatility looks ``calm,'' tempting a BUY from narrative-style reasoning vs.\ a HOLD from a risk gate; and \emph{(iii)} Moral Trader sometimes amplifies API or data-integrity notes, producing a third axis that does not track A/B mechanically. Defense, energy, mining, and tankers were chosen partly to force those regimes to appear in one batch.

\section{System Architecture}

\noindent\textbf{Flow (plain language).} For each ticker the pipeline builds one structured market payload (prices, volatility, optional news summaries), fans that payload out to three strategy branches that run concurrently, then passes all three structured outputs into a final evaluator agent. No strategy sees another strategy's text during the decision pass; only the evaluator compares them. Artifacts are written per ticker plus a run-level summary and an optional historical scorecard.

\subsection{Orchestration logic}
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Market data (no LLM):} Python services pull multi-window OHLC-derived features from yfinance and optionally attach Alpha Vantage \texttt{NEWS\_SENTIMENT} (one request per symbol per run when configured).
  \item \textbf{Strategy agents (LLM):} three AutoGen \texttt{AssistantAgent} instances per ticker, each with its own system prompt, run in parallel and return structured JSON (decision, confidence, justification).
  \item \textbf{Isolation:} all three receive the same market JSON; they do not exchange messages before evaluation.
  \item \textbf{Evaluator (LLM):} consumes the three structured outputs and emits agreement flags plus narrative comparison.
  \item \textbf{Persistence:} one JSON bundle per ticker, a cross-ticker summary file, and an optional deterministic backtest file for the assignment bonus.
\end{itemize}

\section{Patterns of Agreement and Disagreement}

\textbf{Where they agreed.} Three-way unanimity appears \textbf{only on GOLD} (all SELL, confidences 8/8/5). Both graded agents and Moral read drawdown plus turbulence as defensive exit; this is the single line in \texttt{summary.json} with \texttt{agree: true} for all three.

\textbf{Where they disagreed.} \textbf{PLTR:} News HOLD vs.\ Volatility and Moral SELL---Moral assigns maximum confidence (10) while flagging empty news and macro beta. \textbf{TTE:} full triangle BUY / SELL / HOLD: News chases momentum language (and again cites a phantom sentiment score), Volatility now \textbf{SELLs} on ``rough path'' / overbought framing, Moral HOLD on empty articles. \textbf{LMT:} News HOLD vs.\ Volatility and Moral SELL on turbulence narrative despite moderate $-5.5\%$ month. \textbf{FRO:} same BUY / SELL / HOLD pattern as TTE with momentum vs.\ defensive vol language.

\textbf{My read (what drives divergence).} The dominant driver remains \textbf{empty news} plus \textbf{inconsistent fallback rules}: Strategy~A still fabricates or stretches sentiment when fields are null (TTE, FRO), while Strategy~B often converges on SELL using vol-path templates that sometimes \textbf{contradict} the sign of \texttt{pct\_change\_30d} (FRO). Moral Trader tracks governance notes and beta language but can emit confidence extremes (PLTR 10, LMT 1) and occasional field errors.

\section{Failure or Surprise Case}

\textbf{Primary failure (unexpected output):} \textbf{TTE} (and \textbf{FRO}) still cite positive ``sentiment'' or narrative where \texttt{avg\_sentiment\_score} is null and \texttt{articles\_count = 0}. That remains \textbf{fabricated grounding} relative to the payload.

\textbf{Secondary weakness:} \textbf{LMT} evaluator \texttt{analysis} mis-describes Moral as HOLD though JSON says SELL; Moral text also mis-states $\beta$ vs.\ the numeric \texttt{beta\_proxy\_spy}. Hard schema checks or post-validation would catch this before submission narratives.

\textbf{Positive surprise:} \textbf{GOLD} shows clean three-way SELL alignment with coherent risk-off language across agents---the one case where empty news did not prevent convergent de-risk framing.

\section{Reflection}

\textbf{Which graded strategy for \$10{,}000 over one month?} I would follow \textbf{Volatility Averse (Strategy~B)}. This run showed Strategy~A can drift from supplied fields when news is empty (TTE), while Strategy~B stayed anchored to explicit turbulence and drawdown checks on PLTR/FRO. For capital preservation over a short horizon, I prefer a rule-like risk gate I can audit numerically, even when it is conservative.

\textbf{A third hybrid (best of both).} Same as prior revision: \emph{(1)} blend only when articles and scores exist; \emph{(2)} if news is empty, forbid free-form sentiment; \emph{(3)} validate evaluator text against structured decisions (would fix the LMT mismatch). This run proves why: four of five tickers are multi-way splits under identical feeds.
